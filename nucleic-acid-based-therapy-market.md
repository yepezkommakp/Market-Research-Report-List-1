<p><p>Nucleic acid-based therapies encompass various innovative approaches for treating diseases through genetic modulation. Antisense oligonucleotides (ASOs) are short strands designed to bind specific RNA sequences, inhibiting gene expression. Small interfering RNA (siRNA) works by silencing genes at the RNA level, preventing the production of harmful proteins. Messenger RNA (mRNA) therapies deliver genetic instructions to cells, enabling them to produce therapeutic proteins. Each type offers unique mechanisms for addressing genetic disorders and other conditions, advancing personalized medicine.</p></p>
